3. The Emotional-Spring Theory suggests friendships stretch or compress due to positive and/or emotional events occurring within relationships – very similar to how springs respond to different physical forces applied upon them (compression or extension).
4. The Distance-Tension Theory describes situations where distance causes tension in relationships – much like how table springs regain a prolonged stretch after being compressed by a significant weight or pressure exerted over a period, relationships also recover after distance triggers accumulated tension over a certain period of time.
